# Resources and Links: Safety and Security Standards

This document provides comprehensive resources for learning about automotive safety and security standards.

---

## Official Standards (Purchase Required)

### ISO 26262 - Functional Safety

**Official Source:** [ISO.org](https://www.iso.org/standard/68383.html)

- **ISO 26262-1:2018** - Part 1: Vocabulary
- **ISO 26262-2:2018** - Part 2: Management of functional safety
- **ISO 26262-3:2018** - Part 3: Concept phase
- **ISO 26262-4:2018** - Part 4: Product development at the system level
- **ISO 26262-5:2018** - Part 5: Product development at the hardware level
- **ISO 26262-6:2018** - Part 6: Product development at the software level
- **ISO 26262-7:2018** - Part 7: Production, operation, service, and decommissioning
- **ISO 26262-8:2018** - Part 8: Supporting processes
- **ISO 26262-9:2018** - Part 9: ASIL-oriented and safety-oriented analyses
- **ISO 26262-10:2018** - Part 10: Guideline
- **ISO 26262-11:2018** - Part 11: Guidelines for semiconductors
- **ISO 26262-12:2018** - Part 12: Motorcycles

**Cost:** ~$200-400 per part (or ~$2000 for complete set)

---

### ISO 21448 - SOTIF (Safety of the Intended Functionality)

**Official Source:** [ISO.org](https://www.iso.org/standard/77490.html)

- **ISO/PAS 21448:2019** - Road vehicles — Safety of the intended functionality
  - Note: Currently a PAS (Publicly Available Specification), full standard in development

**Cost:** ~$150-250

---

### ISO/IEC 8800 Series - AI Safety

**Official Sources:**

- **ISO/IEC TR 5469:2024** - Artificial Intelligence — Functional safety and AI systems
  - [ISO.org Link](https://www.iso.org/standard/81283.html)
- **ISO/IEC 23894:2023** - Information technology — AI — Guidance on risk management
  - [ISO.org Link](https://www.iso.org/standard/77304.html)
- **ISO/IEC 24028:2020** - AI — Overview of trustworthiness in AI
  - [ISO.org Link](https://www.iso.org/standard/77608.html)
- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** - AI Management System
  - [ISO.org Link](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html)

**Cost:** ~$100-200 per standard

---

### ISO/SAE 21434 - Cybersecurity

**Official Source:** [ISO.org](https://www.iso.org/standard/70918.html)

- **ISO/SAE 21434:2021** - Road vehicles — Cybersecurity engineering

**Cost:** ~$200-300

**Related SAE Standards:**
- **SAE J3061** - Cybersecurity Guidebook for Cyber-Physical Vehicle Systems

## Recommended Learning Path

For someone new to automotive safety and security:

1. **Start with:**
   - MIT OpenCourseWare Self-Driving Cars (free)
   - Read "Safety Assurance for Autonomous Vehicles" (free PDF)
   - Watch Lex Fridman interviews with AV leaders

2. **Deep dive:**
   - Take Coursera Self-Driving Cars Specialization
   - Study NIST AI Risk Management Framework
   - Join LinkedIn groups and follow industry news

3. **Professional development:**
   - TÜV or SAE certification courses for ISO 26262
   - Purchase and study relevant ISO standards
   - Attend escar or SAE World Congress

4. **Hands-on:**
   - Use CARLA simulator for scenario testing
   - Contribute to open-source AV projects (Autoware, Apollo)
   - Practice HARA and TARA on real systems
